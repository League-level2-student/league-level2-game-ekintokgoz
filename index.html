Crossyroad

by: Ekin Tokgoz

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because I wanted to create something that others and I could enjoy to play. I also wanted to challenge myself with a game that seemed hard to make.

2. What I Learned

I learned that the train noises in my game should play a little earlier so players have a warning that the train is coming. I also changed the movement so you are not able to hold down the arrow key.

3. Architecture of My Game

In my game, there are roads that spawn two cars with a random width and speed. I also have a train track that spawns a train on it. There are trees in the game blocking your way.

4. Technical Implementation

Something that I am proud of is the train noise dinging 200 before you reach it and then the train comes.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project that I faced was my movement and collision box. To fix my collision box, I made it so that my code would have a predicted box and if it didn't collide, my player would be drawn.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

One improvement I would like to make is to fix the slight delay on the character movement. Something that I would like to add in the future would be a river with logs to jump on.
